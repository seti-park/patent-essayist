# Example

Tesla's answer: **8-bit MACs**.

MAC (Multiply-Accumulate) is the fundamental unit of neural network computation. Multiply, then add. Most of AI inference is this operation repeated billions of times.

8-bit MACs consume roughly a quarter of the power of 16-bit MACs. They take a quarter of the die area. They generate less heat. You can pack four times as many into the same chip.

> "Lower bit-width data paths reduce power consumption and circuit area." (US20260017051A1)

**8-bit isn't a limitation. It's an optimization for Physical AI.**
