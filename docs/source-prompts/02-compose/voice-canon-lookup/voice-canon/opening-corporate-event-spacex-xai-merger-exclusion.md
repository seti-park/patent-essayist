# Example

On February 2, 2026, SpaceX and xAI officially merged into a single entity valued at $1.25 trillion. In the memo announcing the deal, Elon Musk described the combined company as:

> "the most ambitious, vertically-integrated innovation engine on (and off) Earth, with AI, rockets, space-based internet, direct-to-mobile device communications"
> (Elon Musk, SpaceX-xAI merger memo, February 2, 2026)

Tesla was not part of the deal.

Analysts weighed in immediately. Some saw this as Musk distancing Tesla from his other ventures. Others called it strategic protection for Tesla shareholders. Bloomberg had reported days earlier that SpaceX considered merging with Tesla. But when the deal came, Tesla wasn't in it.

The consensus: SpaceX and Tesla are going in different directions.

Then I started reading patents.
