# Rank speculative product mappings by the filing's own text, not by meme value

When an investor edition reaches the "what is this really for?" section, order the
candidate readings by how much textual support the patent itself gives them, and
lead with the ones the document names. Confirmed on the US 2026/0158546 A1 digest
(2026-06-12): the first draft led with the Optimus question; SETI redirected to
aerospace + consumer electronics because (a) both are literally in the [0059]
application list while a robot never appears under any wording, and (b) the
SpaceX adjacency was tierable that week (SPCX IPO priced 2026-06-11, NPR; merger
odds quoted by Wedbush/Ives vs Kalshi via CNBC and Yahoo Finance). Market context
of that kind goes in fenced and sourced ("none of that is inside the filing"),
with falsifiers; unnamed-product speculation (e.g. Optimus) gets at most a
one-line dismissal that notes its lack of textual support. SETI's stated taste:
do not force the humanoid angle.
