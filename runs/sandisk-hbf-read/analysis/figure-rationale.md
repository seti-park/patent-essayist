# Figure rationale (figures_locked)

The set is chosen to carry the column/row symmetry that is the essay's spine, with a real
before/after on the column axis and the row-axis counterpart beside it.

- **Figure 1 (orientation, header)** — '143's GPU-plus-eight-HBF-packages drawing situates the
  whole essay: this is memory built to sit around an accelerator and be read. Placement: header,
  image-plus-caption.
- **Figure 2 (mechanism)** — '143's NAND string is the one primer the lay/investor reader needs:
  bit line on top (the column), word lines as the stacked gates (the rows), unselected rows held
  at read-pass VREAD. Everything after refers back to it. Placement: body, image-plus-caption.
- **Figure 3 + Figure 4 (contrast + evidence, progressive pair)** — '885 FIG. 9 vs FIG. 10 are
  the before/after of the column-axis trick: bit lines that dip-and-recharge between reads vs
  bit lines held flat at ~0.2 V. They must stay together. Placement: body, image-plus-caption,
  side by side.
- **Figure 5 (evidence)** — '143 FIG. 8A is the row-axis counterpart to Figure 4: unselected
  word lines flat at VREAD across two reads. Placed next to the column pair so the symmetry is
  visual, not just asserted. Placement: body, image-plus-caption.
- **Figure 6 (mechanism)** — '143 FIG. 10's flowchart shows the mode is *triggered* by a detected
  read burst, which is the sophistication beat the moat section needs. Placement: body, caption.

Locked for the run: list, roles, and order do not change during revision unless a Phase-3
finding explicitly calls for it.
