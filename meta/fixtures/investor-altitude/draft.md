# The Patent Behind Starlink's Next Market

## The bar it has to clear

Starlink's next act is becoming a global mobile network. That market has one brutal rule: the connection cannot drop while you move and satellites hand you off every few minutes.

![How a request travels.](figures/fig-01A.png)

*From your dish, up to a satellite, down to a ground station, and onto the internet.*

## A dish that saves itself

Each dish is handed two separate routes that share nothing. When one breaks, the dish jumps to the other without stopping to diagnose what failed. It just steps onto a path built in advance to be clean.

![Two separate routes from one dish.](figures/fig-05A.png)

*Two routes from one dish, through different satellites and different ground stations, sharing nothing.*

## Why it is the moat

For ships, planes, and phones in motion, reliability is the product. Surviving a failure locally in a fraction of a second, without waiting on anything central, is what makes planet-wide coverage possible.
