# Example

A Cybercab, photographed from behind in a Tesla showroom, presents a continuous gold surface. The curve of the roof extends back and down to the rear bumper without interruption. Two thin black horizontal bands cross the rear. One sits just below the upper edge of the trunk lid. The other runs along the top of the bumper. Small red square reflex reflectors sit at the lower corners.

No brake lights are visible. No turn signals. No taillights. The two black bands read as shut-lines between body panels, the kind of seam that exists on any modern car but is not itself a light.

Then a second photograph. A matte-finish prototype Cybercab with a Texas manufacturer plate, in daytime city traffic. The two horizontal bands sit in the same positions as before, with the same proportions.

This time the bands emit red light, in discrete segments rather than continuously. The upper band lights in segments visible toward the far side of the car. The lower band emits at each side of the license plate slot and again at the outer corners.

**The lights were in those bands the whole time.**
