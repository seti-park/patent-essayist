# Thesis Spine

## Selected thesis

**One-line spine**:
> For an investor pricing Agility's IPO, the defensible technology in US 12,560,948 B2 is not how
> the robot walks but how it comes to rest near a person, the graduated, clearance-aware,
> center-of-gravity-lowering safe-stop that gates un-caged deployment, and that is a real but
> bounded moat.

## 4-axis grounding

### Axis 1 — Claims anchor
> Claim 1 locks a rising-risk sequence: decelerate, then determine clearance, then reconfigure
> "based at least partially on the second hazard information and the clearance information,
> wherein reconfiguring the mobile robot includes lowering a center-of-gravity of the mobile robot."

### Axis 2 — Problem anchor
> `[0010]` "Such robots are always falling to some degree during normal operation." / "If a
> dynamically stable robot loses power or otherwise becomes disabled, it collapses." +
> `[0013]` segregation "greatly reduce[s] the productive potential of dynamically stable robots."

### Axis 3 — Effect anchor
> `[0029]` fall-extent reduction "by at least 30%, by at least 50%"; `[0047]` "collapse in a
> predictable manner and into a compact form"; `[0032]` "increasingly greater losses of efficiency
> while providing increasingly greater risk reduction."

### Axis 4 — Baseline-difference anchor
> Industrial baseline = E-stop that cuts power (fall for a biped) and/or a cage (kills
> productivity); prior art (Scianca 2021, Takubo 2007, Zhou 2016) covers pieces, not the claimed
> graduated clearance-aware COG-lowering combination.

## Adversarial defense

**Strongest objection**: It is one independent METHOD claim in a crowded field. Method claims are
harder to detect and enforce than device claims, and a single independent claim is one validity
target, so is this actually a moat rather than a thin filing?

**Mitigation**: The claim tracks the physics of the problem, so the cheap design-arounds (skip
the clearance check, freeze instead of lowering the center-of-gravity, collapse the escalation to
one threshold) reproduce the exact danger the Background describes and yield a worse robot
(`[0010]`, `[0013]`, `[0033]`). It reaches wheeled forms (`[0015]`) and is drafted in the language
of industrial stop standards (`[0041]`), and it sits in an active family (co-pending 19/067,693 +
PCT). The verdict is scoped accordingly: strong evidence of focus and competent fencing, not a
market-locking patent.

**Residual risk**: Acknowledged. The enforcement/validity limits are real but bounded; they scope
the moat rather than cancel it. NOT to be presented as equal-weight counterweights (this is the
firm-closing guard below).
