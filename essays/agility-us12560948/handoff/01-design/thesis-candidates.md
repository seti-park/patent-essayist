# Thesis Candidates

Single-spine default. Three candidates generated from invention-summary Layer 4 angles; one
selected, two rejected with rationale.

## C1 — "The moat is the safe stop, not the walk" (SELECTED)

- **Frame**: For an investor pricing the IPO, the defensible technology is not locomotion but the
  graduated, clearance-aware, center-of-gravity-lowering way the robot comes to rest near a
  person, which is the capability that gates un-caged deployment.
- **Layer 4 source**: angle-deployability-moat (+ angle-escalation, angle-cog-lowering).
- **4-axis**: all four anchored (claims `[claim 1]`; problem `[0010]`/`[0013]`; effect
  `[0029]`/`[0032]`; baseline E-stop/cage + prior art). Complete.
- **Q7**: `corporate-narrative-friction` — the $2.5B IPO narrative sells capability; the patent
  fences the stop. Specific event (SPAC/AGLT), measurable friction.
- **Why selected**: directly answers the investor's question (moat or not); maps cleanly to an
  admitted hook; lets the claim-scope discipline carry the rigor.

## C2 — "You can't switch off a balancing robot" (REJECTED → folded into body)

- **Frame**: technical-impossibility entry — a humanoid can't be safely E-stopped because cutting
  power makes it fall, so how do you stop it safely?
- **4-axis**: anchored, but Axis 4 (baseline-difference) overlaps C1.
- **Q7**: `technical-impossibility` — valid, but a general-reader hook, less investor-decision-
  relevant than C1.
- **Rejection reason**: strong mechanism hook but does not by itself answer "is there a moat."
  Folded into C1's §"What The Body Forces" as the problem setup (the reader's "you can't just
  switch it off" intuition), not used as the spine.

## C3 — "From capability to safety: the company grew up" (REJECTED)

- **Frame**: the storytelling-series arc (the newest filings shift from new tricks to safety as a
  maturation signal).
- **Rejection reason**: this is the bundled storytelling brief's angle, not investor-decision
  content; weaker Q7 anchor (an abstract trend, not a specific narrative-event friction); risks
  the speculation/maturation framing the investor-edition lesson warns against. The maturation
  observation may appear as at most a supporting sentence, not the spine.
