# Figure rationale

Per-figure purpose and intended reader effect.

## FIG. 1 — the robot (establishing)
- **Purpose**: show the body the safety problem is about: a tall, top-heavy, dynamically balanced
  humanoid with arms, legs, end effectors, feet.
- **Intended effect**: the reader sees why a power-cut E-stop is dangerous before the argument
  says so. The shape is the premise.

## FIG. 3 — the decision tree (mechanism)
- **Purpose**: render claim 1's escalation as a diagram: "should I decelerate?" through "is it
  safe to reconfigure?" to a safe operating stop, with hazard information feeding each gate, a
  clearance-information input to the reconfigure step, and hold-and-watch states.
- **Intended effect**: the moat in one image. The reader sees the escalation and the clearance
  gate that claim 1 locks, not a single stop threshold.
- **Caption anchor**: `[0033]` (why reconfiguration can be the wrong call when there is no time).

## FIG. 6 — the kneel end state (payoff)
- **Purpose**: show the post-reconfiguration pose: compact kneel, knees and end effectors on the
  ground, center-of-gravity dropped.
- **Intended effect**: makes "lowering a center-of-gravity" concrete; the reader sees the
  compact, predictable-on-fault posture the claim is buying.
- **Caption anchor**: `[0047]`.
